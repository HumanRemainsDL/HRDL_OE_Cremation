Phoenix 38b–49b Translation from C Jones 2012

The leaves under the breeze are never corrupted, nor does the flame ever harm them— as it was before the change of the world occurred. When the majesty of the water, the sea-flood covered all of middle-earth of old, the circle of the world so that noble plain, altogether perfect, stood steadfast against the heaving way of the rough waves blessed, unspoiled, through the mercy of God. It endures blossoming until the coming of the blaze, of the judgment of the Lord, when the death-halls, the shadowy coffers of men, become unclosed.
